UofT

The University of Toronto is a globally recognized research university and has a huge prestige
on global educational markets. It comprises three campuses located in Scarborough, Mississauga,
and Downtown Toronto. With students from over 159 different countries and regions, U of T is
one of the most diversified institutions within Canada. It offers over 700 undergraduate and
300 graduate programs in a wide range of fields, which ensures that there is a suitable
program for everyone. The university has secured a top ranking in Canada for several consecutive
years and continues to be the hub of innovation, diversity, and knowledge.

- former prime minister introduced universal health care, the Canada Student Loan Program, the Canada Pension Plan, the Order of Canada, and the Maple Leaf flag, he unified Canada's armed forces, convened the Royal Commission on Bilingualism and Biculturalism, and kept Canada out of the Vietnam War, Et Cetera, after returning from Oxford he taught at UofT

John Charles Fields

- founded the Fields Medal, lectured at UofT

- medical scientist, physician, painter, and Nobel laureate noted as the co-discoverer of insulin, studied medicine at UofT
